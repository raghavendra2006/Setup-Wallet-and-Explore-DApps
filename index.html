Distributed Ledger Technology

Unlike traditional databases controlled by a single entity, a distributed ledger is replicated across thousands of computers (nodes) worldwide.

  • Immutability: Once recorded, data cannot be altered without consensus from the network
  • Transparency: All participants can view the entire transaction history
  • No Single Point of Failure: The network continues even if individual nodes go offline
  • Trustless: No need to trust a central authority — math and code enforce the rules

Consensus Mechanisms

Consensus mechanisms ensure all nodes agree on which transactions are valid.

  • Proof of Work (PoW): Miners solve complex puzzles to validate blocks. Used by Bitcoin. Energy-intensive but battle-tested
  • Proof of Stake (PoS): Validators lock up (stake) cryptocurrency as collateral. Ethereum moved to PoS in "The Merge" (2022), reducing energy usage by ~99.95%
  • Delegated PoS: Token holders vote for delegates who validate transactions

Smart Contracts

Smart contracts are programs deployed on the blockchain that automatically execute when predetermined conditions are met.

  • Immutable: Once deployed, code cannot be changed (though upgradeable patterns exist)
  • Deterministic: Same inputs always produce the same outputs across all nodes
  • Gas Fees: Every computation costs "gas" — a fee paid in ETH to compensate validators
  • Languages: Primarily written in Solidity (Ethereum) or Rust (Solana)

Gas Fees & Transactions

Every operation on Ethereum requires gas — a unit measuring computational effort.

  • Gas Price: Measured in Gwei (1 Gwei = 0.000000001 ETH). Higher gas = faster confirmation
  • Gas Limit: Maximum gas you're willing to spend on a transaction
  • Transaction Fee: Gas Used × Gas Price. Simple transfers cost ~21,000 gas
  • EIP-1559: Introduced base fee + priority tip model for more predictable pricing
  • Testnets: Gas fees on testnets (like Sepolia) cost nothing — perfect for learning!

Cryptographic Principles

The math that secures wallets, transactions, and the entire blockchain.

Click to learn more →

Cryptographic Principles

Blockchain security relies on several cryptographic primitives:

  • Hash Functions (SHA-256): Convert any data into a fixed-length string. Even tiny changes create completely different hashes
  • Public-Key Cryptography: Your wallet has a public key (your address) and a private key (your secret). Only the private key can sign transactions
  • Digital Signatures: Prove you authorized a transaction without revealing your private key
  • Merkle Trees: Efficiently verify large sets of data by organizing hashes in a tree structure

02 — Setup

MetaMask Wallet Setup

Follow these steps to install and configure your first crypto wallet. MetaMask is the most popular Ethereum wallet, used by millions worldwide.

01

Install MetaMask Extension

Navigate to the official MetaMask website and install the browser extension.

Download MetaMask
Security Warning: Only download MetaMask from metamask.io. Never install from third-party sources — malicious clones exist that will steal your funds.
  1. Visit metamask.io
  2. Click "Download" and select your browser (Chrome, Firefox, Brave, or Edge)
  3. Click "Add to [Browser]" in the extension store
  4. Pin the MetaMask extension to your toolbar for easy access
02

Create Your Wallet

Set up a new wallet and securely back up your recovery phrase.

  1. Open the MetaMask extension and click "Create a New Wallet"
  2. Create a strong password (min. 8 characters). This password protects your wallet on this device
  3. MetaMask will display your Secret Recovery Phrase (12 words)
  4. Write these words down on paper in the exact order shown
  5. Confirm your recovery phrase by selecting the words in the correct order
CRITICAL — Seed Phrase Security:
  • Never store your seed phrase digitally (no screenshots, no cloud storage, no notes apps)
  • Never share it with anyone — no legitimate service will ever ask for it
  • Store the written copy in a secure, offline location
  • If someone gets your seed phrase, they can steal all your crypto
03

Configure Sepolia Testnet

Switch MetaMask from the Ethereum Mainnet to the Sepolia test network.

  1. Click the network dropdown at the top of MetaMask (shows "Ethereum Mainnet" by default)
  2. Click "Show test networks" toggle (if not visible, go to Settings → Advanced → Show test networks)
  3. Select "Sepolia" from the list of available networks
  4. Your MetaMask should now show "Sepolia" in the network selector
  5. Your balance will show 0 SepoliaETH — we'll fix that in the next section!
Why Sepolia? Sepolia is the recommended Ethereum testnet. It mirrors the Ethereum mainnet environment but uses valueless test ETH, making it perfect for learning without financial risk. Goerli is being deprecated.

03 — Fund

Obtain Testnet ETH

Faucets are services that distribute free testnet tokens. You'll need test ETH to pay for gas fees when interacting with DApps on the testnet.

01

Copy Your Wallet Address

  1. Open MetaMask and ensure you're on Sepolia network
  2. Click your account name at the top to copy your address
  3. Your address looks like: 0x1234...abcd (42 characters starting with 0x)
02

Request Testnet ETH from a Faucet

Choose any of these reliable Sepolia faucets:

Google Cloud Faucet 0.05 ETH / day Alchemy Faucet 0.5 ETH / day Infura Faucet 0.5 ETH / day PoW Faucet Mine testnet ETH
  1. Visit a faucet (some may require account creation)
  2. Paste your MetaMask wallet address
  3. Complete any captcha or verification
  4. Click "Send" / "Request" to receive test ETH
  5. Wait 15-30 seconds for the transaction to confirm
03

Verify on Etherscan

  1. Check your MetaMask balance — it should now show SepoliaETH
  2. Visit sepolia.etherscan.io
  3. Paste your wallet address in the search bar
  4. You should see the incoming transaction from the faucet

Transaction Anatomy

Transaction Hash Unique identifier for this transaction (like a receipt number)
From The faucet's wallet address that sent you ETH
To Your wallet address (the recipient)
Value The amount of ETH transferred
Gas Fee The cost paid for processing this transaction
Block The block number where this transaction was included

🧪 What is a Testnet?

A testnet is a parallel blockchain that mirrors the mainnet but uses worthless tokens. It's a sandbox where developers and learners can experiment without risking real money.

💧 What is a Faucet?

A faucet is a web service that distributes free testnet tokens. Since testnet tokens have no real value, faucets help you get started quickly.

🔍 What is Etherscan?

Etherscan is a block explorer — a search engine for the Ethereum blockchain. It lets you look up any transaction, address, or block in real-time.

Uniswap — Decentralized Exchange

Uniswap is the largest decentralized exchange (DEX) that allows you to swap tokens without intermediaries. It uses an Automated Market Maker (AMM) model instead of traditional order books.

1

Navigate to Uniswap

Visit app.uniswap.org. The interface will detect you're on the Sepolia testnet automatically.

2

Connect Your Wallet

Click "Connect Wallet" → Select "MetaMask". A MetaMask popup will ask you to approve the connection. Review the permissions and click "Connect".

3

Set Up the Swap

Select ETH as the token you're swapping from. Choose a token to swap to (e.g., USDC or DAI). Enter a small amount (e.g., 0.01 ETH).

4

Review & Confirm

Click "Swap". Review the transaction details including:

  • Exchange rate & price impact
  • Estimated gas fee
  • Minimum received amount

Confirm in MetaMask by clicking "Confirm".

5

View Transaction on Etherscan

After the swap confirms, click the transaction link to view it on Sepolia Etherscan. Save the transaction hash for your submission!

OpenSea — NFT Marketplace

OpenSea is the world's largest NFT marketplace. On the testnet, you can browse, mint, and list NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens) to understand how digital ownership works on the blockchain.

1

Navigate to OpenSea Testnet

Visit testnets.opensea.io — this is the testnet version of OpenSea.

2

Connect Your Wallet

Click the wallet icon and connect your MetaMask. Ensure you're on the Sepolia network.

3

Browse NFT Collections

Explore the testnet marketplace. You'll see test NFT collections. Browse through listings and understand attributes, traits, and pricing.

4

Understand NFT Concepts

Minting: creating a new NFT on the blockchain. Listing: putting an NFT up for sale. Transfer: sending an NFT to another wallet address. Each of these actions creates an on-chain transaction.

🔍 Key Things to Check

  • Status: "Success" means the transaction was processed correctly, "Failed" means an error occurred
  • From/To: Verify these match your wallet and the DApp's contract address
  • Value: The amount of ETH transferred in this transaction
  • Gas Used: How much computation the transaction actually required
  • Transaction Fee: Total cost = Gas Used × Gas Price

06 — Protect

Security Best Practices

In Web3, you are your own bank. There's no "forgot password" or "call customer support." Learn these practices to protect your assets.

CRITICAL

🔐 Seed Phrase Protection

  • Write it on paper, never digitally
  • Store in a secure, fireproof location
  • Never share with anyone — EVER
  • Consider using a metal backup for fire resistance
HIGH

🎣 Phishing Prevention

  • Always verify URLs before connecting wallet
  • Bookmark legitimate DApp URLs
  • Never click wallet-connect links in DMs
  • Double-check contract addresses on Etherscan
HIGH

✅ Transaction Verification

  • Read MetaMask popups carefully before signing
  • Verify the contract address you're interacting with
  • Check token approval amounts — revoke unlimited approvals
  • Start with small amounts when trying new DApps
MEDIUM

🛡️ General Safety

  • Use a hardware wallet for significant holdings
  • Keep software updated (browser, MetaMask)
  • Use separate wallets for testing and real assets
  • Enable browser security extensions
